Project Ideas:

                Overview:

My dream is to make a game. However making a game requires a lot of skills, resources and we as university students are still lacking a lot of those requirements. Luckily there are other kinds of games that we can potentially make that use less resources like the once famous mobile game “Flappy bird” or other simple games on the top chart of the App Store/Google Play(Rigney, 2014). So my plan is to create a simple mobile game that we can put on Google Play for free. As of right now I couldn’t settle on the name of the game just yet but I already have ideas of gameplay and other aspects of the game planned.

        Motivation:

        As I said, making a game is my dream and in order to achieve that dream I need experiences, skills so making a mobile game is a suitable alternative. In addition, the cost of making a mobile game is not a lot(Rigney, 2014) while generating a lot of revenue. For example, there was a very famous game a few years ago called “Subway Surfer” which cost around $30k while earning almost $450k only in 2018 alone(Fan Studio, 2018). Additionally, mobile phones are almost a necessity in today’s world so in theory there are about 7 billions potential consumers and the majority of them have access to the Internet(Lin, 2021).

                Description:

        This game is considered a mobile, dungeon crawler, bullet hell, 2d top down and roguelike game. In this game, we focus more on gameplay rather than story however the story aspect is still our second most important. Being a 2d game we don’t need to focus so much on technical issues but we will make sure that the game won’t overheat the player’s phone and consume any data when they are not connected to wifi.

                

                Gameplay:

        Gameplay is a very important factor as this is the only way we can keep the players from playing once then forget about it. The gameplay loop starts with the player spawning in a room choosing their first perks. Perks are upgrades to the player’s character that have many benefits from increasing their health pool to turning them to an overpowered werewolf/similar humanoid creature with some drawbacks. After choosing the perks, players will have to move, using a joystick like system in the lower part of the phone, to the next room where they will face slimes, goblins and some other kind of weak monsters as their first enemy, the difficulty will increase each room the players pass through. The players will have to fight through various rooms until they reach a boss room where defeating the boss will reveal treasures(perks or items) and a way to the next floor. Along the way to the bossroom there will be ways that lead the player to a treasure room, containing items like armour or weapons which will change the player’s playstyle. While moving through floors, the players will encounter a shop randomly and trade in game currency to buy perks and items. A blacksmith will also appear randomly between floors to make the items/perks that the players obtained better. Perks and items will only last for the duration of the chapter the player is in.

        There will be 2 game modes at the launch of the game however if the game is somewhat successful we will add more contents into the game overtime. The first gamemode is the story mode where the player can experience stories with around 5 chapters and each chapter contains 10 floors with different themes, in addition each floor contains 6 procedurally generated rooms with varied roomsized. Finishing every 3 chapters, players will unlock more perks and items that will be added to the perks and items pool in endless mode. Finishing a story will award the player with a new playable character with different designs and attributes that will interact with the items and upgrade differently. At launch, there will be 3 stories and depending on the state of the game the first 2 months we will plan whether or not we should add more contents. The other game mode is an endless mode where the player will travel through floors themed and face monsters corresponding to the stories they are from endlessly or until their character dies. In endless mode, players have lives that correspond to the amount of characters they have maxing out at 3. There will also be a leaderboard system inside the endless mode where the players compete with each other to see who can go further by synergising different items and perks.

                Story:

        In this game, each story will have about 3 parts to it and the first story will be different to the next. For example, the first story will be about a boy who had fallen through a hole which led him to an underground kingdom and he had to navigate his way up with the help of the players. During the journey, the boy will encounter some friends and events, like betrayal or conflicts, to keep the player from getting bored. There will be simple cutscenes to convey the story at the start of the chapter and at the end of the chapter. Every floor there will be some simple dialog about the character’s pasts and feelings which will change further into the story. The next story will be about a girl climbing up to heaven to ask the gods about her missing brothers. However some floors on heaven are corrupted so there will be temptation to lure the girl in so that she will fall into the underground kingdom. The third story will reveal some simple twists and it will be an adventure of 2 characters finding each other with the help of a third character. In the end of the third story the player will unlock the third character.

                

                Technical issues:

        From my personal experience of playing mobile games, my phone tends to get too hot despite it being a new phone so one of the aspects for us to focus on in terms of improving technical issues is to prevent overheating the phone. In order to fulfil that, we decided to limit the frame rate to 30 frames per second(fps) since running a 2d game at 60fps while gameplay might be smooth the overall results seems to be inefficient because 60fps will result in hurt the player’s phone as it push the system to overdrive which led to the phone heating up. The game will be online when the player’s phone connects to the internet however if there is no connection the game will switch to offline mode. Offline mode means that the player won’t have access to any items in the in-game store that require premium currency which can be bought with real money.

IT Technologies:

        Cloud computing:

Cloud computing to my understanding is having computing system resources(often is data storage) without having them tangible. For example, an Iphone with a storage capability of 64gb however in order to increase the amount of storage one usually has to buy a new phone with higher capability. However cloud computing allows customers to in theory save their money and real life spaces. For example, rather than having to buy another Iphone one can utilize Apple’s iCloud to save storage space in the phone instead the datas are stored on the servers in the data center in this case Apple’s data center. Cloud computing also allows employees to work on the same project more efficiently since every input of every employee involved will automatically be stored in the data center as well. For example, Google docs is one the cloud computing applications and it helps teams to work together on the same file without much demand.

Cloud computing is getting better and better since there is news in the gaming scene that we can now play games using cloud technology(Henderson, 2021) which means that one no longer needs to own a high-end computer setup to play games smoothly. In the future, it would be very likely that one only needs to own a phone to enjoy PC games given how far cloud computing has evolved. I believe that if cloud gaming becomes successful it won't be far until we will be making AAA games or a hollywood grade movie using only our phone perhaps in the next three years.

        The invention of cloud computing has heavily impacted most employees and especially people who primarily work on computers. Cloud computing has helped employees from losing their work because they forgot to save or unfortunate events occur like a blackout. Instead, employees can focus more on the quality of their work since it will be saved automatically even if someone accidentally closes the program that the work is on. Cloud computing has also helped me a lot. Projects and reports have been easier ever since I discovered cloud computing because I don’t really have to worry about saving so I saved a bit of time to think about the content of the work I’m doing. My friends have also started to use cloud computing applications like google docs and some even use cloud computing programs exclusively which I don’t really mind since it makes teamwork somewhat more fun because we can all work on the same file and don’t have to pass around save files.
